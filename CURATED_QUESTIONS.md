# Manually Curated Multiple Choice Questions

To prevent poor answer combinations, the following **12 questions** have hand-picked wrong answers that are contextually appropriate:

## Government Structure Questions (Q13, Q14, Q17, Q18, Q37, Q42)

### Q13: Name one branch or part of the government
**Correct Answer**: Congress, Executive, Judicial, President, Senate, House of Representatives, Supreme Court
**Wrong Choices**: Congress, the Senate, the Executive
**Why**: All are governmental bodies/branches - students must know which specific one

### Q14: What stops one branch of government from becoming too powerful?
**Correct Answer**: Checks and balances, Separation of powers
**Wrong Choices**: Separation of powers, The electoral system, State governments
**Why**: All government control mechanisms - students must understand checks and balances specifically

### Q17: What is the judicial branch of government?
**Correct Answer**: the Supreme Court, Supreme Court
**Wrong Choices**: the Senate, the House of Representatives, Congress
**Why**: All governmental bodies but different branches - tests knowledge of judicial vs legislative

### Q18: What does the judicial branch do?
**Correct Answer**: Reviews laws, Explains the Constitution, Resolves disputes between states
**Wrong Choices**: Makes laws, Enforces laws, Collects taxes
**Why**: All governmental functions but different branches - tests understanding of judicial role

### Q37: What does the judicial branch do?
**Correct Answer**: Reviews laws, Explains the Constitution, Resolves disputes between states
**Wrong Choices**: Makes laws, Enforces laws, Collects taxes
**Why**: Same as Q18 - duplicate question with same answer patterns

### Q42: What is the highest court in the United States?
**Correct Answer**: the Supreme Court, Supreme Court
**Wrong Choices**: the House of Representatives, the Senate, the Electoral College
**Why**: All Federal institutions but different types - tests knowledge of court hierarchy

## Constitutional/Historical Documents (Q28, Q51, Q75)

### Q28: What does the President do?
**Correct Answer**: Commands the military, Makes laws, Enforces laws
**Wrong Choices**: Enforces the laws, Commands the military, Signs treaties with other countries
**Why**: All presidential functions - tests understanding of specific executive powers

### Q51: Name one important document from American history
**Correct Answer**: Declaration, Constitution, Bill of Rights, Mayflower Compact, etc.
**Wrong Choices**: Constitution, Bill of Rights, Declaration of Independence
**Why**: All foundational American documents - tests recognition and knowledge

### Q75: What happened at the Constitutional Convention?
**Correct Answer**: Constitution was written, The Founding Fathers drafted the Constitution
**Wrong Choices**: Founding Fathers wrote the Constitution, George Washington presided, Debates about representation
**Why**: All related to Constitutional Convention - tests specific event knowledge
